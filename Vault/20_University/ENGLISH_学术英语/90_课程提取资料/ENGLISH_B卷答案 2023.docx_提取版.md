Reference Summary：

For more than forty years, the cost of food has been increasing because of modern industrial agriculture: mechanization, mass use of chemical fertilisers and pesticides, monocultures, battery rearing of livestock, and genetic engineering. The yields of produce have been rising sharply, but it has caused great damage to the environment.  

Professor Pretty concludes that our food bills are higher than most people realise because we make three different types of payment and that bringing it down will be one of the great challenges of the 21st century. He feels it is realistic to suggest that Britain should reduce its reliance on intensive farming. 

Although most farmers would be unable to adapt to organic farming, Professor Pretty suggests the government should initiate change by establishing what he refers to as a Greener Food Standard, which would push consumers and farmers towards a more sustainable environmental practices than the present ones.
