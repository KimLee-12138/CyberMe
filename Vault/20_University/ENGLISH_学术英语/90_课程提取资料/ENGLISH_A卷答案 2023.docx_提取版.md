Reference Summary：

Human beings are blessed with the natural heritage: forests. The decline of Europe’s forests over the past 15 years has led to a growing concern about the serious imbalances which threaten them. There has been a growing awareness of the need for countries to get together to co-ordinate their policies. In December 1990, the first ministerial Conference was hosted on the protection of Europe’s forests.                                  .

Generally speaking, European countries consider forests important for they perform biological, economic and recreational functions. Therefore, a real concern arises throughout Europe about the destruction to the forest environment, which endangers these three fundamental roles .

So far primary forests are becoming increasing few because they have been changed for thousands of years. Consequently, a forest policy is vital and it must be made. A general declaration was made so that the full potential of these forests could be maintained, which was followed by  six detailed resolutions to assist national policy making.
